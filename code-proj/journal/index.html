Game Design Research

May 28th 2026 : 2:30pm

I personally play a lot of different types of games. They’re fun, enjoyable, and allow me to become immersed in these vast worlds and stories; this is all possible with good game design. According to Sabqat Ruba’s article, “7 Essential Game Design Principles”, the main purpose of game design is to design exciting game features, both graphical and technical, and to provide a good user experience. The main design principles listed includes:

  • Set Clear Goals and Objectives
  • Engaging Core Mechanics
  • Maintain Game Flow and Player Experience
  • Balancing the Game
  • Offer Feedback and Rewards
  • Playtesting
  • Sound Design

Good game design strives to make an experience that is engaging with the user, to excite, not bore; not frustrate, but challenge. Game design is a balancing act and it’s up to the designer to make the constraints and functionalities of the game clear to keep the player engaged, thus including rewards, creating ambiance with sound, and keeping a consistent game flow. All of these elements help to create this seamless gameplay experience.

I thought game design resembles basic UI/UX in a lot of ways, but trying to provide visual clarity to users and using familiar design elements in order to guide the users to their desired actions.

Visual Thinking Strategies Research

May 4th 2026 : 6:30pm

I think Michael Gonchar’s article, “10 Intriguing Photographs to Teach Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills” helps to illustrate the importance of intention with our visuals.

The concept of visual thinking is to slow down and really focus on what an image tells us, without a caption telling us what to think about it; how it relates to the content, what the image tells us, and why. And as designers, we should be keeping these things in mind when we add visuals and images into our own designs. How to engage with the audience, what does the image tell them about the content in my design, and to spark curiosity within the viewer. Images can be very powerful, unintentionally or not.

A good example is “San Rita”, a creative studio website. In their site, they utilize interaction and images to visually represent their creative process. On their about page, they state, “We treat every project like a new terrain to map”, and their entire site’s mechanics and visuals take inspiration from this, themed as a terrain map. The images used are all images of the team outdoors, nature, and their work.

I think this site and has many strengths including:

  • Visual consistency
  • Intriguing images & interactions
  • Visually balanced

I think the site’s images and interactions add a lot to the design. The images inspire a sense of freedom, inspiration, and creativity due to its connection to the theming. They allow for a look at who the designers are and a glimpse into their work as designers too. However, I think the design suffers from overwhelm. Because there is so much interaction and because the mechanics are not standard, I think the user can easily become lost and confused regarding what the site is and how to navigate it.

Overlays Design Pattern Research

April 23rd 2026 : 3:30pm

Modals and overlays can be very useful, but must be utilized wisely by the designer. According to Naema Baskanderi’s article “Best Practices for Modals / Overlays / Dialog Windows”, these overlays are useful for grabbing the user’s attention, procuring user input, showing additional content, and information unrelated to the parent page.

However, if used badly or designed poorly, it can quickly annoy or frustrate users. Baskanderi suggests some tips for good modal design, including always including an escape button, use a descriptive title for the overlay to provide context, button label clarity, use appropriate sizing and location, focus the overlay by using the lightbox effect, make sure these overlays are always user initiated, make your overlays accessibility-friendly, and avoid using overlays on mobile designs (Baskanderi, 2017).

All these practices allow for context for the user, clarity, and focus. All this in the hopes to not interrupt the user flow too much while also simplifying our content and presenting it to the users in a helpful and efficient way.

I thought this article helped to articulate the best ways for me to design overlays and how exactly I should be thinking and incorporating them into my own design work. Additionally, it helped to emphasize the importance of clarity and user flow because of the nature of overlays. Overlays themselves already somewhat disrupt the user, and the article helps to explain the need for context when switching between the parent page and the modal.

Research Form Design

April 12th 2026 : 3:15pm

Based on the article by Salim Ansari called “Best Practices for form design”, I learned the importance of well-designed forms. Little details within the structure of a form can affect your form’s accessibility and can be the difference between a form’s completion and abandonment of the form.

One of the biggest factors for form design is cognitive load. Too much content and steps on screen can quickly overwhelm the user, making it difficult to complete the form. Ansari states that some of the best tips include minimizing input fields, chunk the form into steps, answer easy questions first, and use only one column. These layout tips allow information to be conveyed in a digestible manner, allowing users to focus and makes it easier to finish the form.

Another major tip was clarity. It’s important for readers to understand and see everything that is required of them. Confusion or vagueness can easily turn to frustration that harms the form’s usability. So, making sure all options are shown, marking requirements, and specific language makes the experience that much more bearable.

I thought Ansari’s article gave out great general information for basic form design and I intend to keep them in mind when I do my own design work. I think a great example of form design is the signup page for HelloFresh. In their design, the signup and checkout are listed through chunking with a progress bar, reducing the needed information to be displayed one at a time. Additionally, the prompt is always on screen and error information is clearly stated. The questions are clear and the form itself is designed to be less overwhelming for the user.
